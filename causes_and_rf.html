Leading Causes Of Diabetes:

With conditions like diabetes, there isn't a single cause for the disease. Instead, a combination of factors contribute to a diagnosis. However, there are some key factors that are more commonly associated with the development of diabetes. See them below!

Both type 1 and type 2 diabetes have a strong genetic component. While genetics alone do not determine whether someone will develop diabetes, they can significantly increase an individual's risk.

Type 1 diabetes is primarily the result of an autoimmune response, where the body's immune system mistakenly targets the pancreas, leading to a decrease in insulin production. Although environmental factors may play a role, the condition is mainly influenced by this autoimmune process.

About 5 to 9 percent of pregnant women develop gestational diabetes, which appears during pregnancy even if there were no prior signs of diabetes. Fortunately, this type is usually temporary and goes away after the baby is born.

Major Risk Factors of Diabetes:

Rather than talking about the leading causes of diabetes that you can't control, we'll talk about some unhealthy behaviors that you could fix by yourself!

Being idle can actually increase your chances of getting diabetes! The more active you are, the more efficient your insulin becomes, which helps reduce the risk of diabetes.

Obesity is one of the largest contributers to getting diabetes. The extra fat interferes with your body's response to insulin. It also stresses your pancreas and causes harmful hormonal imbalances. The growing rate of obesity in America is also causing diabete's rates to go up as well!

With poor sleep comes low energy levels, hormonal imbalances, and even weight gain! All of these increase your risk of obtaining diabetes, especially when they continue for too long.
